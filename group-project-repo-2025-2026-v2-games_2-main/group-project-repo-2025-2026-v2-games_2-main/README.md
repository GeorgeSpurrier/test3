# Uni Night Shift

> A first-person survival–puzzle game set within the University of Hull library, reimagined as a dark, atmospheric space after hours.

## Product Vision

Uni Night Shift is a first-person survival–puzzle game set within the University of Hull library, reimagined as a dark, atmospheric space after hours. The game takes place late at night, with the library abandoned, lights dimmed, and an unsettling presence lurking among the shelves.

The player begins on the top floor of the library and must work their way downwards, floor by floor, with the ultimate goal of escaping the building. Progression is guided by environmental exploration rather than linear objectives. On each floor, the player must locate a computer terminal with a blinking status light, indicating an interactive system required to unlock access to the next level.

Interacting with these computers presents the player with quiz-style questions based on books they have previously taken out of the library. These questions are optional in terms of progression—answering them incorrectly will not prevent the player from advancing—but correct answers reward the player with power-ups that provide advantages on the floors below. This design choice ensures accessibility while still encouraging engagement with the educational and narrative elements of the game.

The primary threat within Uni Night Shift comes from possessed books that roam the library. These enemies introduce a unique movement mechanic: they only move when the player moves. If the player remains still, the enemies freeze in place. This creates a tense, strategic experience where players must carefully plan their movement, balance risk and reward, and remain aware of their surroundings at all times.

The combination of exploration, light puzzle-solving, and psychological tension aims to transform a familiar real-world location into an unsettling and immersive game environment. Uni Night Shift blends academic themes with horror-inspired mechanics, offering a distinctive gameplay experience that reflects student life, memory, and the pressures of university in a creative and engaging way.

# Task 1 – Introduce vision and pitch idea

## Task 1.1 – Project vision and pitch idea

As a group we have decided to improve the current University of Hull onboarding experience; we plan to do this by creating interactive onboarding experiences (games). However, due to the time constraints of this project, we have focused on delivering one complete interactive game for **Brynmor Jones Library**. This is overlooked in the current onboarding experience but is influential to a student's university journey.

**Uni Night Shift** is designed to be a unique onboarding experience; a single player comedic horror game set in the library after closing hours in which students have full access to each floor. Students have **10 minutes** to learn the layout of each floor, and what services are on offer.

Progression is linked to short multiple-choice questions related to what they have learnt about the library; this encourages exploration and strengthens student knowledge of the library. While navigating the library students are pursued by university themed enemies (e.g. library statues). If caught, the student will experience a short jump scare instead of dying or losing progress. This keeps tension low for the player while keeping the experience entertaining.

## Task 1.2 – What is wrong with the current system?

- Information about the library can only be found on the UoH website or Hubble Portal.
- The information isn’t interactive, so students are less likely to engage with it.
- Guided tours might cause anxiety for some students.
- Many students don’t know about the services the library offers.

## Task 1.3 – Why should we solve this issue?

- Help students get familiar with the Brynmor Jones Library.
- Showcase academic and support services in the library (Skills Team, computers/workspaces, book a room, loan books and laptops, café, etc.).
- Reduce anxiety around asking for help and/or going on guided tours.
- Reduce load on student services (Hubble staff) for library-related questions.

## Task 1.4 – Who is involved?

### Target Users
- New students
- Old students
- International students

### Stakeholders
- University of Hull
- Library Staff
- Student Services (Hubble)

## Task 1.5 – User Frustrations

- Students struggle with navigating the library.
- Library information isn’t easily accessible (buried in websites/portal; important services aren’t clear).
- The current presentation isn't engaging (students are less likely to read web pages).
- Students feel anxious asking for help or going on guided tours.

## Task 1.6 – How we plan to solve this?

### Discovery (Research)
- Speak with stakeholders and students on what features to include.
- Study the layout of the library (photos or floorplans).
- Investigate influential people or objects that can be turned into enemies.
- Define the project's vision and goals.

### Alpha + Beta (Development)
- Use Unreal Engine for the development of Uni Night Shift.
- Use user stories to create required functionality for the game.
- Meet with users and stakeholders to make sure features that they want are implemented.
- Play testing.

### Live (Launch Product)
- Launch the finished product.
- Game delivers on all the key user stories.

## Task 3.4 – Professional & Ethical Framework Alignment

Our approach aligns with the **IEEE Software Engineering Code of Ethics**:

- **Principle 1 (Public Interest)**: The game directly benefits student wellbeing by reducing anxiety around library navigation and provides equal access to library information for all students (new, returning, international).

- **Principle 2 (Client & Employer)**: The game supports the University of Hull's strategic goals for improved student onboarding and reduces pressure on limited Hubble staff resources, allowing them to focus on more complex student issues.

- **Principle 3 (Product Quality)**: Rigorous playtesting with target users, accessibility audits (WCAG 2.1 compliance), and quality assurance across multiple platforms ensure the game meets professional standards.

- **Principle 4 (Integrity)**: We make transparent, defensible design decisions (e.g., choosing statues over staff as enemies for ethical reasons) and openly communicate limitations and scope constraints to stakeholders.

- **Principle 5 (Management)**: Our team follows ethical project management practices with regular stakeholder consultation, clear documentation, and planned post-launch support and updates.

- **Principle 6 (Profession)**: By documenting our ethical decision-making and accessibility approach, we contribute to industry best practices and demonstrate that student projects can meet professional standards.

- **Principle 7 (Colleagues)**: We collaborated respectfully with library staff and student services in design decisions, protecting their professional reputation and incorporating their expertise throughout development.

- **Principle 8 (Self)**: Our team actively learned new skills (Unreal Engine, game design, accessibility standards) and remains committed to reflecting on ethical decisions and improving future practice.

# Rough Plan – Project Vision – Uni Night Shift

## Project Idea
Create an interactive and fun onboarding experience (a game) for students (new and returning) to get familiar with the University of Hull’s Brynmor Jones Library. Players will learn the library’s layout and discover the academic and support services it offers.

---

## What is wrong with the current system?
- Information about the library can only be found on the UoH website or Hubble Portal.
- The information isn’t interactive, so students are less likely to engage with it.
- Guided tours might cause anxiety for some students.
- Many students don’t know about the services the library offers.

---

## Why (Goals / Purpose)
- Help students get familiar with the Brynmor Jones Library.
- Showcase academic and support services in the library (Skills Team, computers/workspaces, book a room, loan books and laptops, café, etc.).
- Reduce anxiety around asking for help and/or going on guided tours.
- Reduce load on student services (Hubble staff) for library-related questions.

---

## Who (Target Users & Stakeholders)
- **Users:** University of Hull students (new and old).
- **Stakeholders:**  
  - University of Hull (business aspects?)  
  - Library staff (Skills Team, café, etc.)  
  - Student support services (Hubble)

---

## What (Original Concept)
> Combine both old and revised “what” ideas into a completed “what”.

- **Uni Night Shift** is a single-player horror–comedy game set in the University of Hull library after hours.
- The player can explore the library across multiple floors while avoiding possessed books that pursue them.
- Computer terminals throughout the building present optional quiz questions based on library books.
  - Correct answers reward the player with power-ups.
- Core threat mechanic: enemies only move when the player moves, creating tension and strategic movement.

---

## New “What” Ideas (Revisions)
- Remove book-based questions; streamline to **one multiple-choice question per floor** of the library.  
  - Example: *Ground Floor Question – What do you need to access the first floor of the library?*  
  - Removes the need for an SQL database linking books to student ID; questions could be hardcoded.
- When a question is answered:
  - The lift allows progression to the next floor and lights come on (enables continued exploration).
  - If answered incorrectly, lights still come on to help the player find the answers (lower tension since this is an onboarding tool).
- Replace floating books with characters from UoH lore:
  - Examples: Wilburforce Cat, the frog statue, other statues, ghost of the library founder, lecturers.
  - If caught: jump scare happens, but the player **doesn’t lose progress**.
  - Adds comedy and helps students learn about influential UoH lore.
- Consider a revised name linking more clearly to UoH and Brynmor Jones Library:
  - Examples: **UoH After Hours** / **The Curse of Brynmor Jones**
